# DATA RETENTION AND IMPACT METRICS POLICY

## Purpose

The E-Station Time Log System supports equitable access to shared computing resources in educational institutions.

Data collected through the system may be used to evaluate resource utilization, support institutional planning, and measure educational impact.

## Data Retention

Institutions may retain operational records for a period determined by local policies and regulatory requirements.

A recommended minimum retention period is one academic year to support reporting, trend analysis, and accreditation activities.

Archived records may be anonymized where appropriate.

## Impact Metrics

The following indicators may be generated from system records:

### Access Metrics

* Number of unique student users
* Number of active users per semester
* Number of participating institutions
* Number of managed computer stations

### Utilization Metrics (Note: Some of the computed metrics are done manually, and may be supported by the app in future release.):

* Total sessions
* Total usage hours
* Average session duration
* Average daily usage
* Peak usage periods

### Fair Access Metrics

* Session distribution among users
* Frequency of repeat users
* Utilization of available computer stations
* Access opportunities provided through shared resources

### Educational Impact Metrics

* Student access to institutional computing resources
* Support for students without personal computing devices
* Resource utilization trends
* Evidence supporting digital inclusion initiatives

## Anonymized Reporting

When publishing reports, presentations, research outputs, or public statistics, institutions are encouraged to use aggregated or anonymized data rather than personally identifiable information.

## Alignment with Sustainable Development Goals

Data generated by the system may support evidence of institutional efforts related to:

* Quality Education (SDG 4)
* Reduced Inequalities (SDG 10)
* Industry, Innovation and Infrastructure (SDG 9)

The system's primary contribution is facilitating equitable access to shared digital resources for students.
